INTERNET HISTORY

Ancient Foundations: Pre-20th Century

Early communication networks include optical telegraphs in the 1790s and Morse's telegraph in the 1830s. These innovations laid the groundwork for rapid long-distance data transmission.

1960s-1970s: ARPANET and Early Networking

The U.S. Department of Defense developed ARPANET in the 1960s for academic and military communication. By the 1970s, packet switching and TCP/IP protocols were established.

1980s: Academic and Research Expansion

The internet spread to universities and research organizations. The introduction of the domain name system (DNS) in 1984 made network navigation more user-friendly.

1990s: The World Wide Web and Public Adoptione

Tim Berners-Lee's World Wide Web (1991) transformed the internet into a global resource. Browsers like Mosaic and commercial services popularized the web for businesses and individuals.

2000s: Broadband, Social Media, and E-Commerce

Faster broadband connections enabled streaming, online gaming, and video platforms like YouTube (2005). Social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter connected billions worldwide.

2010s: Mobile Internet and Cloud Computing

Smartphones and mobile apps made the internet ubiquitous. Cloud technologies supported innovations in AI, online collaboration, and data storage.

2020s: IoT, 5G, and Virtual Worlds

The Internet of Things (IoT) interconnected devices from home appliances to vehicles. Advances in 5G and metaverse concepts promise immersive digital experiences while raising new ethical concerns.

BEHIND THE SCREEN

When you type a website address (URL) like www.example.com, your browser sends a request to a server. This request is like sending a letter, asking for specific information to be delivered to you.

The server acts like a giant library, storing all the information for websites. When it gets your request, it sends the data back to your computer so the website can load.

Every device on the internet has an IP address—a unique number, like a postal code, that helps identify where data should go. Your request travels to the right server using this 'address.'

Instead of storing data on your device, the cloud keeps it on remote servers you can access from anywhere. This makes services like Google Drive and Netflix possible.

Search engines use algorithms to find and rank websites based on your query. They 'crawl' the internet, indexing pages to provide relevant results. Paid ads usually appear at the top.

Secure websites use HTTPS to encrypt your data, protecting it from hackers. Unsecured websites (HTTP) don't offer this protection, leaving your information vulnerable.

IMPACT

Social media platforms and websites collect, exploit, and sell personal data without users' full awareness, compromising privacy and making individuals vulnerable to identity theft and manipulation.

Excessive screen time, fueled by algorithmically curated content, fosters addictive behaviors, disrupts real-life relationships, and contributes to sleep deprivation, anxiety, and social isolation.

The pressure to curate a "perfect" online persona leads to unrealistic comparisons, diminished self-esteem, and increased feelings of loneliness and inadequacy, especially among younger users.

False narratives, conspiracy theories, and fake news spread like wildfire online, eroding trust in institutions, polarizing societies, and even endangering lives, as seen with vaccine and health-related misinformation.

The internet provides anonymity, which emboldens individuals to harass, intimidate, or demean others, leading to severe mental health issues, including anxiety, depression, and, in extreme cases, suicide.

RESPONSIBILITY

Verify Before You Share

Be cautious about what you post. Misinformation spreads fast. Before sharing, fact-check everything. Don't be part of the problem—be part of the solution. Stop the spread of false information!

Protect Your Privacy

Don't give away your personal information. Always be aware of who you're sharing with and what data you're handing over. Use strong, unique passwords, and be skeptical of phishing attempts. Guard your data—your privacy matters.

Respect Others' Boundaries

Your words have weight. Online harassment, hate speech, and cyberbullying hurt people. Think before you comment. If you wouldn't say it in person, don't say it online. Respect is non-negotiable!

Challenge Toxic Behavior

Stand up against hate. If you see something harmful, call it out. Don't let toxic behavior go unchecked. Report bullying, discrimination, or harmful content. Be the voice of reason, not silence.

Engage with Integrity

Stay true to yourself. Don't fake your presence online. Be authentic. The internet thrives on genuine connections—let's keep it real. Avoid fake accounts and misleading identities. Your integrity matters.

Avoid Digital Overload

Step back when you need to. Constant online engagement can affect your mental health. Disconnect to reconnect. Don't let the screen consume you. Your well-being comes first. Take breaks and regain balance.

Stay Accountable

You are responsible for your actions. The internet is a reflection of your choices. Be mindful of your impact. Every post, every comment, every share adds to the bigger picture. Own your influence—make it count.
